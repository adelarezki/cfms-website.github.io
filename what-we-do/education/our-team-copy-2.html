Our Team

The CFMS Education Committee serves the educational needs of Canada’s medical students alongside the CFMS Vice-President Education (VP Ed).

If you have concerns, ideas, or projects you’d like to share please contact both the VP Ed and the pertinent File Lead.

VP Education

  • Tavis Apramian
  • Western University

  • Tavis is responsible for directing the representation, analysis and policy of the CFMS on educational issues. He represents the Federation at such forums where concerns of a primarily educational nature are discussed (listed below). Tavis also undertakes initiatives to enhance the educational experience of members of the Federation.

  • If you have concerns or ideas you would like raised at the below listed organizations, please don’t hesitate to contact the VP Ed: Association of Faculties of Medicine of Canada , Royal College of Physicians & Surgeons of Canada ,Federation of Medical Regulatory Colleges of Canada , and Canadian Residency Matching Service . In addition, the VP Ed oversees ex officio members who represent the CFMS to many external organizations as listed below.

Match Book

  • Co-Chairs
  • Steven Bae (Queen’s)
  • Abdullah Ishaque (Alberta)
  • Member
  • Sunny Xia (Ottawa)

The Annual CFMS Match Book is a joint initiative of the CFMS and CaRMS designed to educate medical students regarding the CaRMS residency matching process and assist with future career planning. As part of the Canadian Federation of Medical Students (CFMS) Education Committee, the Match Book Working Group is tasked with conducting a full review of the CFMS Match Book with the goal of improving the next edition to better fit current medical student needs.

Global Health Advocacy

  • Co-Chairs
  • Marguerite Heyns (Calgary)
  • Kelita Singh (McGill)
  • Member
  • Kaylynn Purdy (NOSM)

The global health education portfolio focuses on leading projects and initiatives in the area of coordinated global health education and advocacy, including working on three major external committees that develop global health education guidelines and competencies. The portfolio comprises of various working groups overseen by the National Officers of Global Health Education, through which committee members contribute to the growing field of global health education across Canadian medical schools.

TTR/MSPR

  • Co-Chairs
  • Cynthia Min (UBC)
  • Members
  • Sneha Raju (Toronto)
  • Bellal Jubran (McGill)
  • Tina Felfeli (Toronto)

The Medical Student Performance Record (MSPR) is an key component of the CaRMS application. One current issue is a lack of standardization in the format of MSPRs across different schools. The CFMS is partnering with the Fédération médicale étudiante du Québec (FMEQ) to evaluate feedback from relevant stakeholders in order to develop formal recommendations that we can submit on behalf of all Canadian medical students to UGME Associate Deans.
